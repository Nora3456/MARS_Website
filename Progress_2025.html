PROGRESS 2025

A note of our progress through the year

Executive Summary

The Astronaut AI Medical Support System project began on September 4, 2025, with the goal of creating a reliable artificial intelligence program that can assist astronauts with medical assessments during space missions. Over the past nine weeks, the team has made consistent progress in research, design, and development, focusing on both the scientific and technical aspects of astronaut health management.

The project has progressed from team formation and goal-setting to active system development and user interface design. Through structured weekly objectives, the team collected and organized data, built and refined the AI model using the Google Gemini API, and prepared materials for the Preliminary Design Review (PDR). After completing the PDR, the team reviewed feedback and began planning for the Critical Design Review (CDR) scheduled for February.

This report provides a detailed written account of the progress made each week, highlighting key accomplishments, challenges, and future goals as the project continues to move forward.

Week 1 (September 4, 2025)


The first week focused on starting the project and organizing the team. Members came together to form the HUNCH AI Development Group and established the foundation for project planning. A detailed to-do list was created, outlining goals, deliverables, and system requirements needed for success. Initial discussions centered on defining what a reliable AI system would look like in a medical and aerospace context. The team also began researching credible sources and data types that would later be used to train the system effectively.

Week 2 (September 11, 2025)


During the second week, the team began planning and structuring the data collection process. Members identified key information needed to simulate patient assessments, including questions related to symptoms, medical history, and other relevant factors. These plans were developed while maintaining strict ethical and procedural guidelines for data use. The team also created a comprehensive document that categorized potential medical emergencies astronauts could face during missions. This document organized conditions based on treatment urgency, from immediate life-threatening issues to long-term health risks. It also explained how space-specific factors such as microgravity, radiation, and isolation can intensify illnesses and complicate treatment.

Week 3 (September 18, 2025)


In week three, the team continued refining its data collection process and began gathering verified medical information related to space-based conditions. Every source was carefully evaluated for accuracy and credibility. This week also marked the beginning of AI development. The team started building the system using the Google Gemini API, which provided access to reliable, verified data. These steps laid the groundwork for creating an AI capable of analyzing medical data and providing trustworthy recommendations.

Week 4 (September 25, 2025)


Week four was dedicated to organization and planning. The team gathered all project materials, research findings, and requirements in a shared workspace to ensure easy access and consistency. During this time, the team reviewed environmental and technical challenges that might affect the system, such as limited computing capacity in spacecraft, microgravity effects, and user interface accessibility. The group also identified potential design risks and testing challenges that would need to be addressed later to ensure system reliability and usability.

Week 9 (October 30, 2025)


In week nine, the team continued working on the user interface and refining the AI system.
